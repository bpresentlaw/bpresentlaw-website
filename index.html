What We Do

Practice areas

  • Start-Up & New Practice Formation

    Comprehensive legal support for healthcare professionals launching a new practice — from entity selection and licensing to lease negotiations, vendor contracts, and employment agreements, so you can open your doors with confidence.

  • Practice Acquisitions & Sales

    Comprehensive representation through the purchase or sale of a healthcare practice, including due diligence, structuring, and closing.

  • Entity Formation & Structure

    Advising on the right business entity for your practice — professional corporations, LLCs, partnerships — and setting up operating agreements that protect you.

  • Employment & Associate Agreements

    Drafting and negotiating associate contracts, non-compete provisions, compensation structures, and employee handbooks tailored to healthcare practices.

  • Commercial Leasing & Real Estate

    Reviewing and negotiating office leases, build-out provisions, renewal options, and real estate transactions for practice facilities.

  • Regulatory Compliance

    Guidance on licensing, state board requirements, HIPAA obligations, and the evolving regulatory landscape facing healthcare providers.

  • Vendor & Supplier Contracts

    Reviewing technology agreements, equipment leases, lab service contracts, and supplier arrangements to protect your practice's interests.

  • Partnership & Shareholder Disputes

    Counseling on partner buy-ins, buyouts, and dispute resolution to protect your equity and your practice's continuity.

About B. Present Law

We understand your practice because we've built our practice around it.

B. Present Law was founded to fill a gap in the legal market: healthcare professionals who needed a trusted legal partner who understood not just the law, but the business of running a clinical practice.

We serve dentists, veterinarians, optometrists, and medical professionals across the country, providing the kind of thoughtful, accessible legal counsel that helps our clients make confident decisions — from their first associate agreement to their eventual sale or succession.

Our approach is straightforward: learn your practice deeply, anticipate legal issues before they become problems, and give you clear, practical guidance when it matters most.
